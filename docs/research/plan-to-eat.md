# Plan to Eat — product & data-model research (2026-07-03)

Research for the mutual-aid rework's recipe book / meal planner / shopping list branch
(see ../archive/mutual-aid-rework-2026-07.md §G/§H). Based on Plan to Eat's help center (learn.plantoeat.com), blog,
extension-store listings, and third-party discussion. Where docs are silent (parsing
internals, default mealtime names), that's flagged explicitly.

## 1. Recipe model

**Fields.** The CSV export enumerates the effective recipe schema verbatim: "Title, Course, Cuisine, Main Ingredient, Description, Source, Url, Url Host, Prep Time, Cook Time, Total Time, Servings, Yield, Ingredients, Directions, Tags, Rating, Public Url, Photo Url, Private, Nutritional Score (generic), Calories, Fat, Cholesterol, Sodium, Sugar, Carbohydrate, Fiber, Protein, Cost, Created At, Updated At" ([Export your Recipe Book](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/export-your-recipe-book)). Note the separate **Servings** (portions) vs **Yield** (total quantity made) — the distinction matters for their Freezer feature ([serving sizes](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/change-the-serving-size-of-recipes-in-your-recipe-book)). Only a Title is required to save a recipe ([How to Add Recipes](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/add-recipes-from-your-own-collection)).

**Ingredient lines are structured, not free text.** Each line has four fields — amount, unit, ingredient name, notes. Their nutrition blog states it directly: "the amount should be in the amount field, unit in the unit field, ingredient name in the ingredient field, and notes in notes," and recommends moving adjectives like "chopped"/"diced" out of the title into Notes for better matching ([nutrition blog, 2023](https://www.plantoeat.com/blog/2023/02/automatically-calculate-nutrition-facts-for-your-recipes/)). The editor is "line-item input" with a "+ Add Heading" button for section headings inside the ingredient list (e.g., "For the sauce") ([manual entry](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/add-recipes-from-your-own-collection)).

**Free-text parsing exists as an input assist.** A "Paste Recipe Text" button accepts a whole pasted recipe and "the program will smartly separate the information for you" into the structured fields ([manual entry](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/add-recipes-from-your-own-collection)). The parsing algorithm itself is not documented anywhere public — only the observable behavior (paste → auto-split into title/ingredients/directions, with per-line amount/unit/item split).

**Organization taxonomy.** Course, Cuisine, and Main Ingredient are three first-class single-value category fields, but they're *user-customizable lists*, not fixed dropdowns — "Add, edit, or remove any of the options" ([Rating, Course, Cuisine, Tags](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/use-rating-course-cuisine-and-tags-for-better-recipe-organization)). Tags are free-form and unlimited per recipe, acting as sub-folders/filters ([What are Tags?](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/what-are-tags)). Rating is a 5-star click-to-save on the recipe card, filterable from the sidebar. A table ("spreadsheet") view shows Source, Total Time, Course, Cuisine, Main Ingredient, Tags as columns ([Table View](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/recipe-book-spreadsheet-view)), and the app tracks per-recipe plan history ("how many times a recipe has been planned and when") ([Recipe History](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/view-recipe-history)).

**Scaling.** Recipes display original ingredients in one tab and a "Scaled Ingredients" tab beside it; you change the "Serves" number and amounts recompute proportionally. The scaled number persists on the recipe until changed again. Crucially, recipe-book scaling does **not** retroactively affect already-planned instances; planner instances are scaled independently, and that per-instance scaling is what drives shopping-list quantities ([recipe scaling](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/change-the-serving-size-of-recipes-in-your-recipe-book); [planner: "only update the ingredients on your shopping list for that one instance"](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/getting-started-using-the-meal-planner)). A global Metric toggle (per recipe view, shopping list, and app) converts *all* recipes and the list to metric display, reversibly ([Metric Conversions](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/metric-conversions)).

**Nutrition.** On-demand ("Calculate Nutrition Facts" button), computed per-serving from the structured ingredient lines against a USDA-derived database ("about 380,000 entries aggregated from multiple sources"), built in-house rather than licensed. Expandable rows show which ingredients contribute to each nutrient total; wrong results usually trace to bad serving counts or adjectives in the ingredient title; users can flag bad values for staff review ([help](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/nutritional-calculation-for-recipes); [blog](https://www.plantoeat.com/blog/2023/02/automatically-calculate-nutrition-facts-for-your-recipes/)). Imported recipes keep nutrition numbers the source site provided ([FAQ](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/plan-to-eat-faq)).

**Extras.** Prep Notes attach to a recipe with a day-offset (0–31 days before cook date), surface bold in the planner's Notes area and as all-day events in the iCal feed; they do *not* move automatically if the recipe is rescheduled ([Prep Notes](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/prep-preparation-notes-on-recipes)). Photos are added by URL or upload ([photos](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/add-a-photo-to-a-recipe)); limited HTML tags are honored in text fields for formatting ([HTML formatting](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/format-your-recipes-using-html)); a distraction-free Cooking View exists ([Cooking View](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/the-cooking-view)).

## 2. Recipe import

Four documented paths ([How to Add Recipes](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/add-recipes-from-around-the-web)):

1. **Recipe Clipper browser extension** (Chrome/Edge/Opera/Brave/Firefox, plus iOS/Android share-extension): opens a side panel with "Import New Recipe"; the imported recipe is editable in the panel before Save, and the panel can stay open while you browse to clip successive pages ([Clipper extension](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/recipe-clipper-browser-extension-beta-website); [Chrome listing](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/plan-to-eat-recipe-clippe/pnlpbkniadcljbhebdjokkkeobenpefj)). Clipped-but-unsaved recipes land in a **Drafts** area (yellow "Unsaved" tag) and are auto-purged after 7 days ([Draft Recipes](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/draft-recipes)). Mobile share-extension clipping covers Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, Pinterest — share the post to the PTE app and it imports (TikTok "take a bit longer, 15–20 seconds") ([Instagram](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/import-recipes-from-instagram), [TikTok](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/import-recipes-from-tiktok-app)).
2. **"Import from Web"** inside the recipe book: paste a URL *or* enter keywords in a search page, then "Import Recipe" on a result and "Save Changes."
3. **Manual entry / Paste Recipe Text** (section 1 above), plus **photo import** in the app — snap/select photos (multi-photo for multi-page recipes, one recipe at a time), extraction starts automatically ([photo import](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/app-import-recipes-from-photos)).
4. **Bulk file import**: Paprika/Recipe Keeper/Copy Me That (html), WP Recipe Maker/RecipeSage (json), Living Cookbook (fdxz), Meal-Master (mmf), MasterCook (mxp/mx2), PTE bulk .txt, PTE .csv — capped at 3,000 recipes per file, with an optional overwrite-on-title-match ([file import](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/import-recipe-files-from-other-programs)).

**Parsing internals are not publicly documented.** PTE's docs never mention schema.org/microdata; the "which websites" article just says "social media apps and your favorite recipe sites all around the web," with unsupported pages handled by emailing support the URL ([which websites](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/which-websites-can-i-import-recipes-from)). Third-party guides describe the general mechanism this class of importer uses — schema.org Recipe markup embedded in the page ([Fond import guide](https://fond.kitchen/guides/import-recipes-from-websites/)) — and PTE's observable behavior (near-universal success on recipe blogs, editable draft before save, occasional failures escalated to support, and imports that arrive with "Servings: 1" defaults noted in their nutrition docs) is consistent with structured-data extraction plus a heuristic fallback. Treat the exact mechanism as unconfirmed.

## 3. Meal planner

**Calendar.** Week and Month views; drag-and-drop is the primary interaction — grab the **handle icon** on a recipe card in the left sidebar and drop it on a day/mealtime; planned items can be re-dragged between days and reordered within a mealtime; unlimited items per mealtime ([Getting Started: Meal Planner](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/getting-started-using-the-meal-planner); [drag-and-drop](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/drag-recipes-onto-the-planner)).

**Mealtime sections.** Days are subdivided into mealtime sections, which can be toggled off entirely ("Display Meal Time Sections") for single-list days ([Planner Options](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/meal-planner-options)). The help center does not name the defaults (observably Breakfast/Lunch/Dinner/Snacks — unverified from docs), and there's no documented rename/add UI; the sanctioned customization is inserting Notes as visual separators inside a mealtime and ordering items around them ([customize mealtimes](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/customize-your-meal-time-sections)). Other settings: timezone, week start day, show-upcoming-weeks-in-month-view, per-serving nutrition/cost totals per day ([Planner Options](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/meal-planner-options)).

**Three plannable event types.** Recipes; **Ingredients** (italicized; appear on planner *and* flow to the shopping list — the non-recipe-item mechanism, e.g. "Bananas"); **Notes** (gray italic; planner-only, zero shopping-list effect; convertible into Prep Notes attached to a planned recipe) ([ingredients & notes](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/add-ingredients-and-notes-to-the-planner)).

**Menus = reusable multi-day plans.** A Menu is a "reusable collection of planned items" — recipes, ingredients, and notes spanning "one mealtime, one day, a full week, or even an entire month." Menus store events *relative to the date range, not specific dates*, so they can be dropped starting on any day; dragging a menu previews per-day item counts, with confirm/undo on drop. Built either in a Menu Editor or by selecting a planner date range → "Save Menu." Friends' non-private Menus are visible/usable ([Menus](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/using-menus-with-the-meal-planner)). Their documented rotation pattern: build a "template Menu" of recurring items (Taco Tuesday note placeholders, weekly staples) and drop it first every planning cycle, then plan around it ([Template Menus](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/creating-template-menus)). There is no documented automatic repeat/rotation scheduler — reuse is manual menu-drops.

**The Queue.** A bookmark subset of the recipe book for "plan soon" recipes — star on the recipe card or "Add to Queue" toggle; shown as a sidebar tab in both Recipe Book and Planner for quick dragging; "Clear Recently Planned" and "Clear Queue" bulk actions ([Queue](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/the-queue)).

**Bulk plan editing, leftovers, freezer.** An "Edit Plan" dialog copies/moves/swaps/deletes all items in a date range (also the path to Save Menu) ([planner overview](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/getting-started-using-the-meal-planner)). "Plan as Leftover" clones a planned recipe onto another day *without* adding ingredients to the shopping list ([leftovers](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/adding-leftovers-to-the-meal-planner)). The **Freezer** stores batch-cooked meals (tracks meals × servings and date frozen); dragging a frozen meal onto the planner auto-adds a defrost note the day before and contributes nothing to the shopping list; removing it from the planner returns it to the Freezer ([Freezer](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/freezer-cooking)). Plans print (optionally with full recipes) and publish as an iCal feed to external calendars ([planner overview](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/getting-started-using-the-meal-planner)).

## 4. Shopping list generation

**Date-range driven.** The list "connects to a specific range of dates on your planner and creates a list from the recipes planned on those dates"; default next-7-days, with presets (today, this week, two weeks) and custom start/end ([Shopping List](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/how-to-use-the-shopping-list)). Changing the range requires connectivity even though check-offs work offline ([offline](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/app-offline-capabilities)).

**Aggregation rules (the important part).** Sort/group hierarchy: Store (alphabetical) → grocery Category (alphabetical or custom order) → ingredient group (a "root ingredient" — e.g. "chopped red peppers" roots to "peppers", "with some adjectives removed") → item title → unit ([current help](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/sort-group-and-combine-items-on-your-shopping-list); [2013 deep-dive blog](https://www.plantoeat.com/blog/2013/09/how-the-plan-to-eat-shopping-list-sorts-and-combines-items-or-how-to-clean-up-your-shopping-list-before-going-to-the-store/)). Merging: "Items with the same title and a matching unit will automatically combine into a single amount." Same title with *different* units does **not** numerically merge — items are "grouped together under a single collapsible line" showing each quantity (their example: "Mayonnaise ¼ cup + 1 ½"). Within US volume units, cups/tablespoons/teaspoons are treated as inter-scalable, but count-vs-weight ("4 potatoes" vs "6 pounds potatoes") and vague units ("pinch" vs "teaspoon") never combine. So **"1 cup flour" + "200 g flour" stays two visible quantities under one collapsed line** — there is no cross-system conversion at merge time (the Metric toggle is display conversion only). Their prescribed fix for stubborn non-merges is manual: edit the two items to have identical title+unit ([2013 blog](https://www.plantoeat.com/blog/2013/09/how-the-plan-to-eat-shopping-list-sorts-and-combines-items-or-how-to-clean-up-your-shopping-list-before-going-to-the-store/)); a merge on/off toggle exists ([Shopping List](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/how-to-use-the-shopping-list)). A permanent fix is **Standardize**: when renaming a list item, a checkbox propagates the new title into *every* recipe containing the old title, irreversibly, so variants ("chicken breast"/"chicken breasts") merge forever after ([Standardize](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/standardize-your-shopping-list-items)). They openly admit "there's often some manual merging or deleting needed" ([Shopping List Hacks blog](https://www.plantoeat.com/blog/2024/01/plan-to-eat-hacks-the-shopping-list/)).

**Categories learn per user.** Category assignment is two-tier: your own history wins ("if you move ketchup into your condiments category, Plan to Eat will automatically place all future instances… until you move it elsewhere"), else a global "most popular category" default ([auto-categorization](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/how-ingredients-are-automatically-placed-in-grocery-categories-on-your-shopping-list)). Categories are user-editable and reorderable (custom aisle order) ([categories](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/organize-your-shopping-list-into-categories)).

**Stores.** Multiple named stores; one "favorite" (heart) store is the default destination for new items; per-item store assignments are remembered for the future; stores collapse/expand for shopping one store at a time ([Stores](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/use-grocery-stores-on-your-shopping-list)).

**Staples list = their pantry substitute.** A *static* side list "of frequently purchased items that can be used as a kitchen inventory" — non-recipe items (paper towels, bananas) with category+store, copied to the shopping list on demand ("Copy Items to Shopping List"); they explicitly recommend it as "a pantry/inventory system" ([Staples](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/use-the-staples-list-for-frequently-purchased-items); [blog](https://www.plantoeat.com/blog/2026/01/get-to-know-the-staples-list/)).

**"Already have it" behavior.** There is deliberately **no quantity-tracking pantry that decrements the list**. PTE shipped a Pantry feature early on and killed it, arguing "removing items from someone's shopping list without them knowing about it is never a good idea," that inventory can never stay in sync with the real kitchen, and that the effort is equivalent either way; the endorsed workflow is "shop at home first" — check off list items you already own before going to the store ([A digital pantry inventory — does it really help?](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/a-digital-pantry-inventory-does-it-really-help)). Checked-off items can be hidden ("Hide Removed" toggle); "Reset List" clears check-offs and manual additions; manual items can be added directly to any category/store; list exports to CSV/text/print ([Shopping List](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/how-to-use-the-shopping-list)) and can be sent to grocery pickup/delivery services ([grocery delivery](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/grocery-delivery)).

## 5. Sharing / social (friends model)

**Symmetric friend graph, browse-not-copy by default.** Friend requests go by email or username (batch up to 100), and become active on acceptance — a mutual relationship ([Invite Friends](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/invite-friends-to-share-recipes)). Once connected, you browse a friend's recipe book live from a Friends tab: only their **non-private** recipes, and only recipes *they* authored/imported — not recipes they themselves saved from other friends (no transitive sharing) ([Accessing Friends' Recipes](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/accessing-recipes-from-your-plan-to-eat-friends)). Menus share the same way ([Menus](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/using-menus-with-the-meal-planner)).

**Live reference until you edit.** While browsing, you see the friend's live recipe — "If you share a recipe with a Friend and you have altered the directions or ingredients, your Friend will also see those changes." You cannot plan a friend's recipe directly; you save it (heart icon) into your own book first. You cannot edit a friend's recipe; choosing "Edit a copy of this recipe" forks it into an independent copy that **breaks the connection** (no further sync, no comment notifications) ([Accessing Friends' Recipes](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/accessing-recipes-from-your-plan-to-eat-friends)). The docs don't state what an unedited saved reference does if the original is *deleted* — not publicly documented.

**Copyright quirk.** For recipes a friend clipped from the web, shared views lock the **directions** until you visit the original site once ("the directions will remain unlocked forever") — a copyright accommodation; ingredients are always visible ([Accessing Friends' Recipes](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/accessing-recipes-from-your-plan-to-eat-friends); see also [Recipe Copyrights](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/whose-recipe-is-it-anyway)).

**Privacy is binary.** Recipes default to "public" (visible to friends + reachable by their public URL, but not search-indexed); "Make Private" hides a recipe from friends, its URL, and social sharing simultaneously — "There is no in-between" (no friends-only-but-no-URL tier) ([Privacy on Recipes](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/privacy-on-recipes)). Friends can **comment** on each other's non-private recipes ([Comments](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/comment-on-recipes)). One-off sharing to non-users sends a *link* (ingredients visible; directions gated by the same visit-the-source rule unless you authored it) ([share via text/email](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/app-sharing-recipes-via-text-or-email)). A curated "PTE Recipes" tab offers opt-in recipes from company-run challenges ([PTE Recipes](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/what-is-the-pte-recipes-tab)).

## 6. Account model, offline, pantry-awareness

- **Single-login accounts; households share credentials.** "We do not offer an option or multiple logins for one account at this time" — the documented household pattern is one shared login on unlimited devices/installs ([FAQ](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/plan-to-eat-faq)). Notably, the Friends feature is the only multi-account collaboration primitive; there is no household/team entity.
- **Pricing:** $5.95/month or $49/year direct ($54.99/yr via Apple), with a 60-day money-back guarantee ([pricing](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/how-much-does-plan-to-eat-cost)).
- **Offline-first mobile app:** offline you can "add, edit and view your recipes, alter your meal plan, and manually add, edit and check items off of your shopping list"; changes sync on reconnect; only login and shopping-list date-range changes need connectivity ([offline capabilities](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/app-offline-capabilities)).
- **Per-ingredient pantry awareness is deliberately absent** (see section 4's pantry rationale) — the Staples list + check-off-at-home workflow is the whole story. One adjacent AI feature exists: "Instant Ingredient Suggestions" in the app proposes substitutions "for allergens, dietary needs, or whatever's missing from your pantry" ([suggestions](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/instant-ingredient-suggestions)).
- **Calendar interop:** planner publishes an iCal feed consumable by Google/Apple/Outlook calendars ([FAQ](https://learn.plantoeat.com/help/plan-to-eat-faq)).

## Design takeaways most relevant to this app

- The load-bearing data-model decision is **structured ingredient lines (amount / unit / item / note) + a paste-parser assist**, since scaling, nutrition, merging, and categorization all hang off it.
- Shopping-list merging is intentionally conservative: **exact title + unit merge, same-root grouping otherwise, no cross-unit-system math**, with user-driven "standardize" renames as the long-term dedup mechanism and a learned per-user ingredient→category(+store) map.
- Planner events are a small closed set (recipe / ingredient / note, plus leftover and frozen variants that *don't* hit the list), and **Menus are date-relative event bundles** — a clean reusable-plan abstraction.
- Sharing is **browse-live, fork-on-edit**, with binary privacy and no transitive sharing — simple and predictable, and their deletion semantics are the one undocumented corner.
- They explicitly rejected decrementing pantry inventory against shopping lists — relevant tension for a pantry-sharing app that *does* track lots: PTE's argument is about silent list mutation, not inventory per se.
